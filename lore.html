The Shattering and the Fall of the Ancient Ones

As recorded by Sehlora Mirinth, Archivist of the First Sun

—

There was a time when the world breathed in harmony.

Before it cracked. Before the light dimmed and we all forgot the name of our gods.

I was young when the Ancient Ones still walked among us, not as kings or tyrants, but as shepherds of wonder. I remember the scent of the Everdawn fields in spring, where flowered grain shimmered beneath golden sunfire and glowing beetles sung between the stalks. We used to laugh, and sing, and dance. That was the world the Ancient Ones nurtured — not created, but guided. They did not shape stone and blood, but values and connectedness. They sang to rivers and they changed their course. They whispered to mountains and calmed their trembling hearts.

Each of them brought something. Not all were gods in the way you might think — not almighty, not infallible. But they were becaons. Powerful. Vast. Iss-Hadal would walk barefoot into the heart of a volcano and coax it into silence. Lei-Sun would place her hand to the bark of an ailing tree and draw life back into its leaves. Wateirov would make us laugh, his illusions showing us the beauty of distant lands.

We mortals were flawed. We squabbled over borders, harvested for selfish reasons, feared each other for our skin, our horns, our wings. But the Ancient Ones never ruled us. They guided us. And through their presence, we grew — cultures blossomed like wildflowers, tangled and diverse, beautiful in their own imperfect colours.

Then came the star.

It fell without warning — no prophecy, no sign. A cold fire that did not burn, streaking across a violet dawn. The day it struck the eastern ridges, everything went dark. We could not see more than a few feet, a misty haze blanketing the world as everything held its breath in fear. We called it the Caliginous Midnight. Every temple bell rang at once, though no hand touched them.

The ground quaked. Mountains folded. Waters consumed. Entire cities crumbled in moments. And in the wake of that mighty tempest, they came - the Oterim - demons from beyond. Monsters of chaos, created by an eternal being of darkness. They tore through our lands, gathering corpses and souls to feed the One. I watched from the balcony of the temple, powerless as they ravaged the cities nearby. I saw the screaming masses, refugees from broken homelands, fleeing from the horror.

Then the Ancient Ones fought back, halting the tide as mortals from every corner of the world rose in defiance. Soldiers, spellcasters, and even the untrained gave their lives to buy time. They held bridges long enough for children to escape. They lit pyres in sacred patterns, delaying the cursed with ancient rites. They fought in circles around their gods, shielding them in flesh so that strategies could be formed, so that one more breath could be taken, one more spell inscribed.

For a time, it worked. The gods rallied. Some struck back with fury, others withdrew to hidden sanctums to restore their essence. Orunyel, Weaver of Futures, unraveled timelines with her golden loom, twisting moments of near-death into chances for survival. Kaelmor Sea-Binder raised entire lakes into the sky, casting them down upon armies of shadow in crushing torrents. And Velthas the Quiet, god of secrets and shelter, erased entire battalions from memory, hiding them in folds of forgotten space until they could strike unseen.

Plans were made. Retreats secured. But faith is a soft thing when placed between the teeth of a devouring darkness. One by one, they were overcome. Some fell with roars that shook the stars. Others offered themselves as anchors for protective spells. And the rest simply… vanished. No prophecy. No farewell. Just silence — and the endless sound of our people running, and falling, and dying, to buy a future they would never see.

In the decades that followed, entire civilizations were lost. The Ra Shemau desert people gone. The quartz-blooded people of the Thal’Ren river, turned to ashes. Millenias worth of learning, crafting, art... forgotten. Names and cultures, lost. Entire species like they never existed.

We — the survivors — gathered beneath the one place that held strong. The massive valley where the Mystical Academy (Czemhaza wa Kanti) offered sanctuary to all creatures and beings. And there, with trembling hands and voices hoarse from grief, we built our final shelter.

Tonwe Khael’Voreen Et - Shelter for the Last Songs

It hurts me so much, to speak of what was lost. But I remember.

And I write it down, so that you remember too.

The Regions Beyond the Walls

Across the centuries, most who speak of Khael’Voreen speak only of the city — its walls, its legends, its radiance. But beyond those sacred borders lies a land as much a part of its survival as the earth beneath its streets. The region surrounding the valley is immense, spanning wilds both untamed and deeply rooted in ancient magic.

To the east lies the Eternal Forest — a vibrant and sacred expanse where the trees tower like cathedrals and the soil hums with ancient power. It is the domain of primal spirits, elusive predators, and ancient tribes. The Cha’Quakka, guardians of life’s balance, have walked its paths for generations. Alongside them dwell the high elves and wood elves, who protect the outer reaches from intrusion. Deep within, the Spirit Mother resides, connecting to the rich diversity of her land.

Northwest, the land sharpens into the jagged rise of the Kor Lodrum mountains — a realm of stone, steam, and subterranean wonder. Within its hollow peaks lies a city forged beneath the earth, where dwarves and goblins coexist in a delicate, stubborn peace. Here, ancient magics meet modern invention. It is a forge not only of blades and armor, but of thought and innovation. Some of the city’s greatest advancements begin beneath this slumbering rock.

To the south, the lush valley opens into a broad spread of frontier farmlands. This is the breadbasket of the region — a land of weathered homesteads, druidic stewards, and settlers who’ve learned to tame wild herds and coax bounty from stubborn soil. Farther still lies the great inland sea, dotted with scattered coastal towns and aquatic settlements. The sea is both lifeline and mystery — its waters rich, its depths largely unexplored.

Northward stands the ancient gate — the only known passage into the valley. Built long ago by hands both mortal and divine, it now serves as a sealed monument. Its towers shimmer with protective enchantments. It has not opened in over four hundred years.

Very few still live within Khael’Voreen that have travelled beyond these lands. Yet together, these four pillars — forest, mountain, farmland, and sea — encircle the last great sanctuary of mortals. They are not wild edges of a map, but living chapters in a story we’ve only just begun to read.

The Heroes Who Shaped the City

While Council, tradition, and infrastructure maintain Khael’Voreen, its soul was forged in the acts of a few — individuals whose deeds shaped the lives of the many. Their legacies are sung in taverns, inscribed in the Academy’s vaults, and honored at the Assembly square.

Caryk Stoneheart is among the most revered. A champion said to have shoulders like a mountain, Caryk led an elite force known as the Red Legion during the twilight of the Shattering. With the passes collapsing and tides of void horrors advancing on the valley, Caryk and his warriors held the northern route for four days and nights. They used illusions, misdirection, and brutal frontline tactics to delay the enemy, buying time for thousands upon thousands of refugees. Caryk was a High Paladin of the god Lornazelud — the light of divine justice. Upon his death, it is said Ancient Lornazelud emerged from the heaves to carry his soul to the beyond herself.

Airelen of the Veil, the Whisper of Stillness, was a high elf enchanter and shadow-walker. During the War of the Shattering, she vanished from the public eye, but whispers spoke of a figure walking battlefields unseen, silencing enemy commanders and turning entire legions upon themselves with whispered commands and perfect illusions. She was the Council's invisible hand, and her very presence was said to still the air. In the final years of the war, she orchestrated the defense of many great cities without ever raising her voice. When the protective barrier was sealed, she was last seen walking into the Eternal Forest, carrying a scroll sealed with ten runes and a single crimson feather..

The Five Heads of Czemhaza remain nameless, their statues encased in a warded ring at the center of the valley. These five were among the greatest mages the world has known — each one a master of a different arcane school, each once a head of the Academy. It was they who came up with the plan the protect the valley with a mystical barrier. Knowing their transient mortality could not sustain it long, they used a mythic enchantment to petrify their bodies, their essence (and the barrier) held in stasis for eternity.

Gruntharc, the Iron Commander, is green-skinned orc in his early fifties. He is Khael’Voreen’s highest military authority and its most formidable warrior. Despite his age, his body shows no signs of slowing, and his name is legend in the battle arenas. His duels against spellcasters and other formidable martial combatants have made him an icon of strength. Guards and soldiers under his command wear his sigil proudly, as he's known as much for his keen tactical mind as his powerful aura.

Torren Sunstrike, the Grand Magus, nicknamed "Wolf-eyes", is the city’s foremost arcane mind. A human of brown-silver hair and sharp blue eyes, Torren has mastered many of the mystical arts. He was one of the key architects behind the Vanguard’s formation and creator of the talisman that now allows travel beyond the barrier. His voice carries weight in all things magical, and his spells have pierced veils that many believed unbreakable. He has publicly denounced the Council on several occasions, accusing them of politcal strategems and greed instead of focusing on the well-being of the City.

Kuranna Vellhart, the Commander of the Northern Watch, is an Aasimar that was born under a rare celestial alignment. Raised within the hallowed halls of Khael'Voreen's grand cathedral, Kuranna serves as an advisor to the Council on matters of spirituality and ethics. She also mentors young clerics and paladins, guiding them in their divine callings. While she is gentle and nurturing, she also possesses a fierce determination when protecting the innocent. Her laughter is said to be as refreshing as a cool breeze, and her wrath, though rarely seen, is likened to a fierce tempest. She is often found standing amidst storms, unscathed and serene.

Whether through sacrifice, vigilance, or silent determination, their stories remain the mortar between the stones, the steady heartbeat of the City and its peoples.

The Council of Khael’Voreen

Beneath the gleaming spires of the Citadel and nestled within the city’s great chambers, the Council of Khael’Voreen is tasked with preserving the legacy of all the cultures in the valley. Each sitting Council member represents one of the city’s 9 major districts, even the Eternal Forest and the halls of Kor Lodrum have representatives. While a few are beloved figures who regularly walk among their people, most are only seen during public decrees and celebrations.

Supporting them is a layered system known as the Circle of Rule. City Lords maintain order within the districts, responsible for civilian life, trade, and stability. They oversee the Guilds — powerful organizations that each manage the lifeblood of their district, from infrastructure to innovation to rations for the citizens. Above them, Ambassadors and Clerks operate between districts, coordinating legislation and managing diplomacy between factions and representatives.

Then there are the Elders — once Council members themselves, they have stepped away from public duties to pursue studies, political manuevering, or long-term projects. Though they no longer have direct power of legistlation, many still hold sway behind closed doors. Some whisper that the Elders control more than any sitting official, and that their influence in shaping the city’s course never truly ends.

Notably, the Vanguard — the chosen few sent beyond the barrier — report not to the Council directly, but to two figures: the Grand Magus and the Iron Commander. While the Council selected the Vanguard’s members and endorsed the project, their day-to-day oversight is more detached… at least, for now.

Magic Without the Divine

In the centuries since the Ancient Ones vanished, magic has not disappeared — but it has changed. Divine spellcasters still heal wounds, shield allies, and smite with holy power. Yet their connection to higher forces is now shrouded in uncertainty. Clerics no longer hear the voices of their gods, and paladins uphold oaths with no reply. Their power remains, but its source is unclear.

Some believe these blessings are echoes — the last whispers of celestial beings long gone. Others insist they stem from within: willpower, commitment, or faith made manifest. Druids, especially those trained deep within the Eternal Forest, still have communion with the Spirit Mother, while others tap into natural forces that have no need for worship. For many, divine connection is not gone, but is distant and unintelligible.

The arcane, by contrast, thrives through study and structure. At the heart of Khael’Voreen stands the great Academy, where mages pursue knowledge across all known disciplines: evocation, illusion, divination, transmutation, and more. It is the safest hub for those who seek to understand magic in a world that is filled with so many questions.

Whether your power comes from ancient rites, forgotten relics, or the strength of belief, know this — in Khael’Voreen, magic is still alive. It has evolved to embrace the silence. Ask your GM how your spellcasting class might interact with these mysteries as you create your character.

The Districts of the Last Bastion

Khael’Voreen is a sprawling city with 9 districts, each a fragment of the world that was, preserved within the walls. Viewed from above, the city forms a great ring around the Citadel, with streets spiraling outward like a sunburst, touching the lives of over 5 million souls.

At the heart lies the Citadel District, home to the towering magical Academy known formally as Czemhaza wa Kanti. It is here that spells are refined, artifacts studied, and magical theory passed down through generations.

The Hammer District is an echo of the Kor Lodrum mines — a place of smoke, flame, and metal. Here, smiths, alchemists, and artificers shape wonders in stone and metal. To the east lies the Emerald Belt, a haven for druids, herbalists, and beast-tamers who dwell close to nature and cultivate the city's connection with the Eternal Forest.

The Watchgate lines the northern wall, where guards keep a watchful eye on the ramparts. It also serves as both fortress and a training ground to the militant forces of the City. To the south stretches the Hearthlands, a district of farmland terraces and colorful markets — a cultural heart where food, music, and history intermingle.

The Shadow Sunward is a radiant district of columns, pyramidal rooftops, and carved obelisks. Originally built in granite by desert-born peoples, its culture is steeped in honor, reverence, and ancestral rites. Across the city, the Nephyrfold Heights rise in elegant layers — a place of orange lantern trees, sweeping roofs, and philosophies that shape sword and spirit alike.

Lastly, the Quiet Belt forms the liminal districts of the city. A blend of forgotten alleys, odd workshops, and forgotten ruins, most citizens consider it uneventful. But those with connections to the undergound know it holds secrets and mysteries... And more.

The Purpose of the Vanguard

Forty years ago, the Council unveiled a new initiative — a declaration of intent, a way to inspire hope and curiosity, paving the way to a brighter future. The Vanguard was to be the first sanctioned expedition beyond the barrier since its creation, a group entrusted with the tremendous task of venturing into the forgotten world and bring back answers.

To the public, the Vanguard are hailed as heroes — a symbol of resilience and rebirth, chosen from among the city’s most gifted and courageous. The city itself is dedicated to providing resources not given to many citizens, and excitement stirs in the hearts for what's waiting to be discovered. Murals have been painted, ballads composed. For a people who have lived behind enchanted barriers for generations, the Vanguard represents a whole city's curiosity and sense of adventure.

Their formation is a process both rigorous and deliberate. Applicants are tested not only on skill, but intuition, resolve, and how they function as a group. Once chosen, the party receives their talisman, a singular artifact called the Al'Karamin. This allows passage back and forth through the barrier, and must be recharged every 8 days in the Academy.

The city eagerly awaits for the applicants to be chosen as the Quincentennial of Khael'Voreen approaches, and the identity of the first recruits are to be announced!
